Lab weeks

III. Reflect

Are you content with your product? And why? Does it achieve what you set out to do?

I’m content with the installation, especially if you’d consider that we started with no clue how to turn on a tiny LED pin and ended up creating a ‘sea of luminescent clouds’. I think our group has really tried its best to breakthrough and make progress along the way. It has certainly achieved what we set out to do, in terms of creating a comfy, dreamy cloud environment, allowing users to interact with sensors to create their favourite combination with lights, sounds and visual patterns. During the Expo, we also received a lot of positive feedback. Our participants did have a great time when they experienced our installation. However, I think it still lacks the element of childhood or inner child. The installation was covered by a blanket and included a floor of pillows underneath. But with how it looks at the end, it feels just like a standard setting rather than the blanket/pillow hut that we would build when we were kids. From my point of view, it is not necessary to illustrate all the concepts with 100% how it looks in real life. But if it is one of the “selling points”, then it should be created in a more concrete context.



What is the quality of your product? Try to quantify/objectively express this.

In terms of the quality of the product, I would say it is decent but can also be improved.
What is in good quality:
• Installation built with a solid and safe structure: Although the walls of our installation were made by two tables, they are actually very sturdy and we tested out by using some heavy materials beforehand. We also added more tough wooden blocks at the bottom, so that the angle of table legs are at the same horizontal view. Before placing pillows, we put a stable pallot for support as well.
• The combination of LED strips with a layer of cotton works extremely well together. It can certainly bring out the dreamy vibe and our participants all enjoyed seeing its variations. The visual patterns also match well with the cloud and the overall environment. As how they look were abstract, it had the effect of creating a different universe.
• Circuit arrangement was highly concerned and therefore we made sure there will not be a short circuit situation.

What can be improved:

• As mentioned in the process chapter, some of our capacitive sensors for changing LED lights did not perform stable. There are a lot of factors that can affect the sensibility, but one thing that we could plan thoroughly is building the circuit that can carry out the best performance. We were not aware of the importance of electronics until the very last moment, thus we couldn’t make adjustments on connecting wires and the position of LED strips.
• The sound environment can be taken further into consideration during the Expo: there were lots of different sources of sound during the expo, therefore it could be hard to listen to our own sound options. I also think that we can be clearer in letting participants know how many and which options they can switch per sensor directly. Otherwise they might feel lost with which sound track they were following.



Reflect on the process that you have gone through and compare your work/effort to that of the group

During these whole lab weeks, I would say that I had been very honest and open to talk about my concerns, strengths & weaknesses, as well as difficulties that I faced with my groupmates. During the first few days, I see myself needing extra time to understand Arduino than my groupmates. I do not take this as an excuse for not learning, in fact, I also tried to catch up and make sure I understood what the issues were. But compared with Yulia and Hanna, they were actively coming up with the main approach and looking for the problems; whereas my role with Arduino was more like assistant and learner at the same time. On the other hand, I'm also proud of myself for finally getting to understand basic electronics more. Back then, I had zero sense with electricity or didn’t even know how batteries work. But throughout the process, I am able to read up more information, get the logic behind and to think along with the group together to look for a solution.
What I took the most responsibilities with were about visuals programming and the installation setup. It was an achievement for me to see how I picked a reference with only a circle to an audio visualizer in the environment, as well as adapting frequency spectrum from the environment in Maakhaven to create a landscape. Although these are the things that were not adapted in our installation, I think I have still gained a lot during the process and have at least tried my best to explore other possibilities on p5js. At the end, I made all 4 visual patterns myself and I used functions that I didn’t even know before lab weeks on 3 of them. Therefore, I would say that I breakthrough a lot in terms of p5js and processing.
In terms of installation setup, I understand that our group has a tight budget and didn’t want to spend too much on the project. Therefore, I took a lot of initiatives to help and collect materials around all different places for free, such as the computer monitor, huge chipboard for attaching LED strips, most of the pillows. During the last few days, I also saw wooden blocks of the right size, sanding wood, soldering wires, preparing capacitive sensors and so on.



How did you spend your time, tell something about this?

On the first day of the lab weeks, I remember I felt very clueless of how to make a start. I spent some time looking for inspiration but I found it one of the most useful approaches, is to think of things that I have never tried, read the basics, follow instructions along but then expand the basics as much as I can. Therefore, during the first week, I really dived into the things that I’m interested in deeply. I also spent quite a lot of time on tutorials. I think the importance of tutorials isn’t really about following, but to understand the logic and be able to apply them in different contexts later on. When I was watching tutorials, sometimes it also involved other functions that I have never encountered. I’d say that it can actually drag you into an endless coding sea :D
As mentioned before, I want to understand what my groupmates were working on and sometimes it might take a longer time for me to achieve that. I'd also bring the issue back home and do research on myself or, of course, ask for an explanation directly.
Not just within the group, sometimes I would also walk to other groups and see what they were working on. At the end we had a lot of sharing of how to do this or that and to give advice to each other. For example, I remember me and Yulia just randomly walked to another group and realized that they were figuring out the relation between resistance and temperature. At the end of the conversation, they provided a useful link for us to look into the concept more.



Were you motivated? Were your expectations met? Did you have a good time? Did you learn a lot? Why? Why not?

I was motivated most of the time! During the lab week, I get to find my own strengths in visuals and contribute in the way that I can. I really wish that I can work more with LED strips but I think everyone has what they are good at and with teamwork we also need to learn dividing tasks in an efficient way.
As for the learning outcome, I would say that I didn’t have the same deep understanding about LED strips and MPR121 capacitive settings in Arduino as Yulia, Hanna and Marissa. We received the MPR board 1.5 days before the expo and I didn't get to experiment with it. It is also more realistic if someone good at it/has been focusing on it to take the initiative to fix the problem. Now thinking back about it, I could have also asked for code sharing and understand more of the setting, rather than just reading up text online. However, as mentioned in earlier paragraphs, I have learned a lot with visual programming and basic knowledge of electronics too.
The expectations that I set at the beginning of this course was “I hope I can develop a nice interactive project that is implemented with different technology, together with other talented students together and with the aim of making the users happy”. To see how our users interact with our installation, I think this aim is successfully achieved :) In terms of technology, our group did try to learn new skills that we never tried before and successfully integrated in the installation. I believe there are even more different types of technology, but I'd say that it was already a challenge with what we did and also a good start for us to dive in more in the future.
I had a good time in general. It feels rewarding to overcome everything till the end! However, there were also moments that I felt not being respected as a team. There was a situation where I recognized some Dutch words and I can tell that they were discussing one of us. I had to look into their faces and be open for conversation. It is a collaboration and if there is something that you prefer to be done in a different way, it should be voiced out and discussed openly but not complaining in your own language. I also requested for sharing resources that everyone has, but at the end I can feel that most people feel very protective of what they “own”. There are certainly some parts that someone took the most responsibilities and it belongs to their own personal work. I can absolutely understand that, but the reason to share was more about understanding the current progress of the team and being able to reflect from there. The collaboration part at the end was disappointing, but it has also given me a lesson of the importance of listing what we should share together as a team, as well as to encourage more open discussion and check in with what frustrations/concerns the team has.
