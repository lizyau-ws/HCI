A. Homeostasis model

Bicycle


1. The needs of the bike to perform its primary function:

a. Stability: it will be gained through forward momentum. Controlling the speed of the bike between a lower and upper threshold is necessary to achieve this need.
b. Direction: Changing direction by steering and simultaneously changing the centre of mass of the bike by leaning to the left or right.
c. Safety/selfpreservation: The need of the bike to survive and avoid incidents is vital to achieve its primary functions. Communicating its intent to other traffic in a complex environment to avoid collisions or incidents is therefore needed.

2. These needs are influenced by:

a. Natural forces such as wind. Balancing the centre of mass to counteract this outside force keeps the bike going.
b. Obstacles. Controlling speed by using the brakes OR changing direction. Braking might cause the bike to go beyond the lower speed threshold forcing the bicyclist to place their feet on the ground. Alternatively, the bike can change directions by steering. The combined centre of mass of a bicycle and its rider must lean into a turn to successfully navigate it. This lean is induced by a method known as ‘counter steering’, which can be performed by the rider turning the handlebars directly with the hands or indirectly by leaning the bicycle.
c. Other bikes and traffic. A bike does not function in a vacuum, but is nearly always using public space. This means using sounds like ringing the bell are needed to operate safely.

3. How the bike behaves like a creature with the model ;)

The fascinating creature known by many as the ‘bike’ has begun its yearly migration towards HHS, facing many dangers and obstacles along the way. First, the bike encouters a heavy gust of wind. Sensing the eb and flow of nature, the bike leans gracefully into the wind, balancing its centre of mass. The wind seems to have distracted a pedestrian who hasn't seen the bike. The bike rings to avoid a collision. Then all out of a sudden, a car steers along the corner crossing the path of the bike. The bike reacts quickly and uses its brakes to slow down the speed, avoiding the car. Finally, the university is in sight and on the right side of the bike. The bike countersteers to the left and changes its centre of mass to the right, thereby successfully initiating the turn. The bike has reached its destination and has satisfied all its needs by balancing its internal state.

B. Research

Texture-changing robot skin

Maker: Engineering students from Cornell University Article link & photos source

Most of the robots we know often appears with a hard or metal skin. However, in this example, the team creates a soft outer skin robot that the texture will be varied based on how the robot itself wants the user to interact with it. Although the idea of social robot isn't an entirely new technology, most of them express its internal state by facial expression and gesture only. The inspiration behind this idea is from observing how human interact with animal species and also how internal states can be seen from skin changes. For example, human gets goosebumps when we feel cold or even terrified; a blowfish becomes spiky when it feels threatened. Therefore, integrating texture changing skin is such a decent details and acts as a new channel for communicating with human.

This idea is only a prototype so far, the team has engineered how the robot works under goosebumps and spikes situation and it is not a fully functionable robot at this moment. Therefore, I couldn't find too much details in regard of how it works technically in a deep level. But I have at least found out how they made the prototype was by using multiple sheets of air pockets that can allow different textures to be generated in various patterns. User can also see the screen of the robot to observe its facial expression to react based upon it.



This example has given me another perspective that us, as a human doesn't necessary to be the prior group to be beneficial. So far we have different social robots or even service that help us improving social life and interaction, yet sometimes we might forget there are other species in the environment or even robots. I'm looking forward to see how this prototype will be expanded and implemented into robots (they have started expanding :D), after all I believe this idea will allow us to gain a deeper understanding and empathy towards objects and environment around us.
